Kyoto

35.0116°N 135.7681°E Visited Spring 2024 5 days

The city that taught me how to slow down

Kyoto does not announce itself. It reveals itself, gradually, to anyone patient enough to walk slowly and look twice. The temples are not tourist attractions; they are punctuation marks in a city that has been writing itself for over a thousand years.

I arrived in spring, when the cherry blossoms were at their peak and every narrow path was packed with people photographing the same tree. I learned to wake at five in the morning. The city at that hour belongs to priests, cats, and anyone willing to trade sleep for stillness.

Fushimi Inari at dawn. Nishiki Market before the vendors set up. The moss garden at Saihoji in soft rain. Kyoto is best experienced as a series of quiet mornings strung together, each one slightly different from the last.
